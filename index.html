More About Me

Based in Sydney, Australia, I am currently an undergraduate research student in games development and a software engineering contractor. I graduated from the University of Technology Sydney, with a Distinction in BSc. Information Technology in 2019, majoring in enterprise systems development and particularly focusing on web and mobile apps, I also have experience working in the software development field. Currently, I am undertaking an honours degree with a research focus in interactive drama systems for video games. I am particularly interested in building interactive drama systems which lean into emergent properties to provide games with valuable and dramatically interesting gameplay features.

In early 2019 I was also invited to present a game narrative review paper I had written at GDC, winning gold for my critical analysis of the game Aviary Attorney as selected by a panel of industry veteran narrative designers. And in 2020 I was named as one of the Australian Financial Review's (AFR) Top 100 Future Leaders, a competition that identifies the most promising graduates across multiple disciplines each year.

I love programming and enjoy spending my free time learning new tools and languages, which has provided me with a great deal of flexibility with regard to the kinds of projects I am able to work on, from games, to networking tools, and even analytics. However I also enjoy (and have experience with) a broad range of creative fields, including: gameplay and narrative design, UX&I design, music production and composition, and video editing.

If you were to ask me about what gets me out of bed in the morning, I would say it's making cool stuff. At my core, I am a creator, and I love to craft experiences, software, games, products, and many more amazing things. In this, I have developed a passion for learning - especially when I can apply it to my craft. So if I'm not working hard at my research, I'm likely at a game jam, learning a new skill, or off hiking somewhere.

GDC Game Narrative Review & Poster Presentation

In early 2019 I was invited to attend and present my work at GDC after winning gold for a paper I had written, as selected by a panel of industry veteran narrative designers.

The paper lays out my critiques of the narrative design of the game Aviary Attorney, applauding it for its use of setting to punctuate the themes of the game, as well as its broad-over-deep approach to the branching narrative. While some of the game's dialogue falls a bit flat, especilly with regards to tone in certain sections, it is altogether a quality experience that deftly illustrates the powers unique to an interactive genre.

At the confrence, I took part in the poster presentation session, where I was able to exhibit a poster that summarises my paper in a visual style to GDC attendees and a select number of professional game narrative designers.
